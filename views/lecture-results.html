Performing cataract surgery in a patient with active uveitis arrow_drop_down

Go to Slide

Kuglen Hooks are the preferred instruments for pupil expansion in these cases, as they allow for very precise and focal pupil expansion in areas where inflammation has led to iris adhesions to the anterior capsule. Furthermore, the hooks can carefully be used to supplement capsular support should zonulopathy occur during any steps of cataract extraction. Ring expanders are far less precise and technically more challenging to implant with synechiae. Scissors and forceps can be used to manipulate the iris tissue, but likely not accomplish adequate pupil expansion. Ahmed segments are used for capsular support in areas of zonular dehiscence and don't have an impact on pupil size.
